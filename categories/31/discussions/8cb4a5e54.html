So, your application was accepted... by FrozenSolstice May 2, 2015 at 10:05 PM UTC

Hey everyone, this is just a helpful little post about dealing with nerves on the day of your Jr Mod interview. I understand some may find it daunting, scary, nerve-racking and more. But these simple tricks will calm your nerves and make it easier on yourself.
[Before the Waiting Room]
1. Be prompt! Don’t rush in at the last minute, you can just be chilling in a public chat until it’s time. Rushing doesn’t calm you!

[In the Waiting Room]
1. Take deep breaths. If your heart is racing, and you feel nervous, take three deep breaths. Breathe in through your nose for 5 seconds, hold it for 3, then let it out for 5 seconds. Repeat the process 3 times. It helps!
2. (Note that this doesn’t work for all people) If you feel scared, blow gently on your thumb. Your thumb contains nerve pathways to your brain that tell you to relax. Try it!
3. Talk. There will be others in the room with you, so why not talk? Don’t blab on about how you’re “going to fail” and “shouldn’t be here”. Talk about anything (appropriate, however ;D) it calms your nerves when you talk.
4. Listen to the Admin/Senior Moderator that comes in every so often. They aren’t there to scare you! They will give important information out, such as who is going next, and other instructions. Listen to avoid the fretting of “What did I miss?” afterward!
5. Have a glass of cool water next to you. You’ll find that your throat goes dry when you’re nervous, so remember to drink when needed!
6. Lastly, go to the toilet. You’d be surprised what nerves do to you, so go to the toilet beforehand! I know I did 5 times!

[In the Interview]
1. This is very important. You have to remove distractions. It could be that pesky Rubix Cube next to you, or a school paper. Leave it, remove it from your sight.
2. Don’t speak to fast! You need your interviewer to be able to understand you. Pace yourself.
3. If possible, try to not have tons of background noise. It can be hard for you to hear your interviewer, and for the interviewer to hear you!
4. I know, this one isn’t totally avoidable, but worth a shot. Try have a secure internet connection. Nothing is more annoying than dropping out during your interview!
5. Be organised. Have things that you might need with you on your desktop/next to you.
6. Last, but most certainly not least, be yourself. That’s all you can do. Be honest and be yourself. If you act like someone you aren’t, it only comes back to nip you later!

Don’t worry if you weren’t accepted. There’s always next time!
I hope this helped!
-Elsa
