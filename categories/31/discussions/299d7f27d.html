The "What if?" Game History Style by Numberz_ December 16, 2015 at 9:12 PM UTC

Hi, it’s been a long time since I made a topic on the forums, so I thought I would introduce a game that I used to play a lot in history class when I was younger. Hope you guys enjoy, I will be using some of the answers you give for future reference.

How it works: You pick one historical event that has greatly impacted the world. You change something about the story so the outcome is different (ex. someone dies or doesn’t).  You write a short scenario of what you think that world today would be like because of this happening/or not happening.

Topics: The topic can be almost anything, from the early history, social media, movies, books.

Example-

Event: 75 BC, Julius Caesar was captured by pirates and holds them off long enough to bargain for freedom.

Change: The pirates kill Caesar.

What our world would be like?: If Caesar died then we would have no Rome Empire cause Caesar wouldn't become dictator, and pass it on to Augustus. Roman territory wouldn’t grow without Caesar leading the legions into Gaul and Brittan.  Shakespeare wouldn’t have been that popular since his most famous plays are based around the life of Julius Caesar. 

Example #2-

Event: Mark Zuckerberg the son of a dentist, goes on to study history, business and comptech, these subjects help him in creating one of the largest social media companies in the world.

Change: Instead of studying history, business and comptech, he follows in his father’s footsteps and becomes a dentist.

What our world would be like?: No Facebook, use of technology/social media would be very different.

Format-

Event:

Change:

What our world would be like?:
