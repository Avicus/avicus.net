Educate yourselves. by Arachadonic September 22, 2014 at 8:09 PM UTC

Recently, I have seen a growing, worrying trend of people throwing around insults (or supposed insults) at each other like it is nothing. In particular what I see is people using words such as "autistic", "down syndrome" and "gay" as insults. I am here now informing you all that these words are not insulting at all, and if you use them as insults or find them insulting then you need to take a deep look at yourself and re-evaluate your sense of morality. So what do these words really mean?:

Autistic: A word used to describe people who fall on the Autism spectrum. Autism is in a group of problems that people can have (adults and children), including Asperger syndrome and others. These issues happen when the brain develops differently and has trouble with an important job: making sense of the world. While this can make hard for anyone who has autism, it is not necessarily a bad thing. It makes you that extra bit unique than everyone else and allows you to see the world a bit different which should be celebrated yet instead, it is viewed as a hindrance or negative thing. 

Down Syndrome: My best friend's little 7 year old brother has down syndrome and let me tell you right now. That little boy is the most loving child I have ever met in my life and I know both his parents and sister would not want him to not have down syndrome as he perfect in his "faults". Down Syndrome is essentially an extra chromosome that these people have (normally humans have 46 but they have 47) and it results in short statue, slightly slanted upwards eyes, and learning difficulties. Again these people have something extra in them that makes them more unique and as with Autism allows them to see the world from a different perspective. Again, nothing wrong with having Down Syndrome. They didn't choose to have it, it just happened!

Gay: This word has recently become globally known with the big move for equal rights for gay or homosexual people to be able to marry their lovers legally. Yet still, people think just as with Down Syndrome and Autism that being gay is a bad thing and therefore is a good insult. As with the previous, people are born Homosexual. They did not choose it and lord knows with the amount of shit they get, why would anyone willingly go choose to go through that? They are literally no different to "straight" people bar the fact that they have different sexual preferences. You may as well use "human" as an insult. 

So, maybe that opened your eyes a little or maybe it didn't and that's okay. All I ask is that you think before you type. There is nothing wrong with being different and rather than put it down we should raise it up! That's it really...
