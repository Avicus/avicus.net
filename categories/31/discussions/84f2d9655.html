Possible solution. by MLGtino July 30, 2014 at 2:07 PM UTC

Everyone hates it when donators join the winning team in a game of Nexus to gain more exp. Well perhaps we can make this change.

So my idea was that the people helping the team the most deserve to be rewarded, and those who cheat don't deserve anything. We can give winning exp based off of how many kills a player has gotten a game. So if you got about 20 kills a game you get like 12 exp when you win, and if you get 0 kills you get nothing. So donators that team swap get no exp. 

It can reset every time someone does /leave to also prevent team stackers. For example, I'm playing Nexus. Then about half-way through I see Smitdalt join the other team. My team has about 5 players, they have 6. I want to be on Dalton's team, so then I switch over and teamstack. BUT I lose my exp with this possible addition.   

Here's an unofficial chart representing what it might be:

3 kills: 2 exp for winning
5 kills: 4 exp for winning
8 kills: 6 exp for winning
10 kills: 8 exp for winning
13 kills: 10 exp for winning
15 kills: 12 exp for winning
18 kills: 14 exp for winning
20+ kills: 15 exp for winning

I'd appreciate the feedback! Tell me what you guys think!
