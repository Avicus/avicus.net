Who will be the new mapdevs? (after the update) We aren't currently sure of the amount of mapdevs that will come after the update. There may be 1 or 2 within the foreseeable future, though, which is before 2015 ends. We'll see.

How many new mapdevs will be there? Possibly 1 or 2, can't speak for the whole team. We've done polls and done meets on this before discussing it among the others. We don't want to have the team expand too much, otherwise it'll be difficult to maintain. 

Regarding yml. What new will be added after the update? I can't say exactly what will be added for YAML, since I don't quite know myself, however it will support some new gamemodes Ivy has worked hard on.

Are there going to be new rotations? We update rotations as we see fit, but after the update rotations will be ready to phase out. A lot of changes will be made, see Ivy's facebook sneak peeks!

More Map Events planned? If we get around to it. Lots of maps in storage at the moment, though broken.

Public yml test server? I don't think this will happen. If it does, I'd imagine you'd have to rent them.

What permissions do mapdevs have? (ingame/forums) In-game, we have commands to set maps, end matches, use build/dev/mapdev servers, and to change cycle durations. We also have a dev chat in-game. On the forum, we have access to staff discussion and a green name. That's about it.

If you could change one thing in the current yml system, what would it be? I'd love for there to be certain items gained after a set amount of kills. For instance, killing a member on the enemy team for the 5th time without dying would give you a special item.

ViceTechnicolour January 11, 2015 at 6:01 PM UTC

What do you look for in a map?
where do you stand on the aesthetics vs gameplay debate?
can you make a thread showing how to make null- regions where you cannot build or place blocks?
It depends. We don't look for anything specific, though gameplay and looks are important. There's creativity, uniqueness, size, and other factors though it's really onto the mapmaker; it's their canvas that we rate.

This is more of an opinion-based thing, but I personally like to find a balance between them. If the gameplay is nice though, there's no questioning I will appreciate that map even if it doesn't look the best.

You just use the build: flag and add a * so nobody can build. Here's an example from Axis;

cyan-protect:
   type: cuboid
   min: -24,66,103
   max: -32,74,111
      flags: build: 
         who: '*'
         priority: <>
         message: <>
