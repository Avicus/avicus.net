Doctrine Idea by Zintenka May 6, 2015 at 9:05 PM UTC

It is clear that the topic regarding posts about personal updates is a confusing one.This is a problem that i've noticed that Avicus staff has. 

You guys do not follow a clear and cohesive doctrine. I suggest that community members help make a clear doctrine for the way punishments and locking posts should be handled.

Community members who aren't necessarily mature but know when to be mature and when to be laid back. Staff members will obviously help but I believe that if the community gets involved as well things will get better. 

Make posts inside server feedback titled [Doctrine] (Topic name). Staff and community members can review. 

Staff, if you want the community to respect you more then you'd agree with this idea and vice versa for community. This must be a truly egalitarian event where everyone must speak to each other as they would if one another were a player.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
WARNING! COMMUNITY THIS IS TO YOU! Try to keep a calm, professional and egalitarian atmosphere. If a heated argument occurs, then you must stop. However, a heated DEBATE is allowed as long as it doesn't occur with insults or actions to slander the opposing party. Deformation of character is a crime in the US.

If neither player adheres to staff warnings then a temporary forum ban for a day must be given out. Nothing more - nothing less.

WARNING! STAFF THIS IS TO YOU! If signs of an argument occur or if an argument occurs then you may not lock the topic. You must warn the offending players to stop because, when you lock doctrine topic:

#1: You look infrac. hungry or "trigger happy" or "power hungry -- whatever the hell you wish to call it.
#2: It won't be fair to the owner of the topic if the shenanigans of two aggressors caused this. Locking a post caused by two or more people is unwarranted and you're thinking of everyone in the topic as one troublesome entity - which is not the case at all so... lay off the cannons and use your words.
#3: Its impractical. 

If a staff member gets into a debate with another player then the arguing staff member mustn't punish the other aggressor. Only staff outside the argument are allowed to warn both to stop. Depending on who started the confrontation, you mustn't be biased. Biased dialogue or actions from staff must be acted upon immediately with a demotion.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

- Zintenka
