The EULA's been updated. by SnowSX3 October 30, 2015 at 3:10 AM UTC

Mojang updated their legal documents ealier today.

Some key points:

Changes to the EULA and Terms of Use
Mojang is now owned by Microsoft, a U.S. company. For that reason, we needed to make some changes to abide by the law and Microsoft’s policies.

The old terms used to say that anyone who caused us legal trouble would have to pay us if the issues cost money to fix. Now, you don’t have to pay us. Instead, new terms say that we’re no longer responsible for any of your damages. 

You can still monetize your servers and videos.

We’re happy for you to make money for your hard work and to feature an exclusivity period for dedicated fans, but we don’t like the idea of permanently restricting Minecraft content to a select group of players. That’s not in the spirit of the game.

Thoughts?
