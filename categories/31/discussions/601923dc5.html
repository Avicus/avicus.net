Is this how we're gonna operate? by Zintenka August 16, 2015 at 7:08 PM UTC

I take a short break for a couple of days and I come back to a friggin cesspool. All the salt dipped on flames. Unfair demotions, arguments all over. Unfriendly relationships and intentions exposed and brought to light...

Is this how we're gonna operate? Are we gonna keep on going like this until we're reduced to 50 players like last time? Probably even 10 players? Or none?

Something has to change. It's everybody's fault this time. Kylo, please update to 1.8 xD We're not gonna get new players if we stagnate for the minority. 

Staff (Moderators):
As a MapDeveloper I observe the other half of the staff team and compare it to my own and the things I see is utterly unprofessional and childish. You guys have created a Clandestine environment for yourselves. Too secretive and distant. If you don't know what I mean take a gander at the CIA, US Secret Service or ONI from the Halo Universe. 

With this type of environment you create a tense atmosphere. You create rivalries and animosity amongst yourselves. You get distracted from your true work as a Moderator and you start to fuck up. Make poor decisions (Apricot's demotion) and you regret them sooner or later. With heavy backlash. 

Stop being too secretive amongst yourselves and be more open and casual among the community. Take a gander at the MapDeveloper Team. We're real friendly with ourselves and we even joke and eff around a lot. A lot of people and staff see me ingame and I actively talk with the community and have fun among you guys. 

Our job as managers of the Repository doesn't get to us and we cope with this by creating an open and amiable atmosphere. 

Moderators, you have to follow this example. Please. You've lost a lot of good people and your credibility is really low right now. I was shocked when Apricot mentioned on his post that you guys told him to act more strict and less casual. This isn't how you build trust and relationships. They may be kids playing but they aren't your kids or siblings. Outsiders don't always respond well to this type of behavior. So be more casual with approaching infractioners... of course, do your job all the same but don't have a cold approach. Have an approach of a calm counselor dealing with an unruly child.

Players:
You guys don't exactly make it easy on the staff as well. When I test certain maps you guys respond with "This map sucks!" and etc. We need actual feedback. What makes the map suck so much? 

And you aren't helping the moderators much either by giving them such a hard damn time. I'm gonna be real honest and tell you guys that y'all can be a real sore and annoying bunch. In fact, you guys help push along the salt of Avicus the most. But when a Moderator effs up or makes a very poor decision I'll admit that it pushes the salt the furthest because it's horrifying to see that these people are the ones with such game-changing commands. But you guys push the salt the most. 

Stop being a thorn in our sides and try to be nicer. It is so awesome and soothes me when I get to do an event with you guys and y'all are so cheerful and full of amiable feelings. It annoys me when you guys are so hostile. Stop it.

Staff (MapDevs):
Allow for players to accept and deny maps. Was suggested by Excellence. If the others like it, we can give this a trial run and see how it pans out in the long run. Only concern is maybe some biased feelings and unfair judgments but I have high hopes for the forum community.

Staff = Be more open and friendly.

Players = Stop giving the staff a hard time.
