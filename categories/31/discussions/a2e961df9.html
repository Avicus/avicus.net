I'm gonna make a little guide explaining what these things mean:

1. You most likely know what megabytes are if you own a computer, but Internet speed is measured in megabits. 1megabyte=8megabits , so my \actual download speed is 1.08 megabyes/sec                                                                                                                                                                                     2. These numbers mean a lot while downloading and viweing/streaming videos but not in gaming. You could have a Gigabit download speed, but still lag because the connection is so choppy. What matters in gaming is how constant your connection is, rather than speed.                                          3. Ping. Ping is how long it takes for a packet of data to reach the destination server and make it back to your computer. You can try pingtest.net  and get this:                                                                                                                                       4. Jitter is the variance in ping while it is testing, so it highly controls lag for games. For example my bing went from 30ms-32ms, so my jitter is 2ms                                                                          5. Packet loss. How many packets are lost in travel if you have anything over 0-1% ya got a problem (like above)                                                                                                                                                                                                                              (you can put this on your first post if you want to)
