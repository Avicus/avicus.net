Lieutenantpants June 15, 2014 at 8:06 PM UTC

I might be able to offer a little insight here. 
First off, I referee'd the last Overcast tournament and am a referee for the upcoming Mini events. 
In the last tournament (Breaking Core), there were only one or two successful rushes. A rusher would be a basically useless position after the first five minutes. 
As for the concept of an all-star team: a little while ago, an Overcast regular by the name of kingoros created an "all-star" team by the name of CoolPVP. It was 45 of the best players on Overcast. They were fantastic. They were skilled. And they disbanded after just a couple of months. Why? Because it ended up being just for fun. Because it would have been utter chaos. You can get a few stars with a bunch of really good people, but the idea of an all-star team isn't practical for something like Minecraft. I'm not saying that it's a bad idea to send a team over to compete in Overcast tournaments, but to just pick "star players" just won't work out in the end for purposes of communication and availability.
