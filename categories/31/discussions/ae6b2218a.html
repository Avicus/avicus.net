Why there is a /report command. by vladthegreat100 January 27, 2015 at 4:01 AM UTC

Hey guys! During the past days as I have been moderating I've seen a decent amount of people accusing of hacks in game instead of using the /report command and then arguing with the staff. On this post I shall be telling you all why we have a /report command hoping you guys will learn and evade any infractions for accusing of hacks.

To start off, the /report command is NOT a command to speak to moderators or to use as a joke to report your friends, anybody caught doing this will result in a probable infraction.

First of all, the /report command is meant to report hackers silently, so they do not find out you have figured out that they hack at the same time notifying all moderators online so we can reach them and deal with them appropriately.

To continue on with the last point, /report isn't only for hackers but for any rule breaker (spammer, scammer, team griefer, advertiser, e.t.c.), so if you find any rule breakers please use /report immediately so us mods can stop them.

Finally to finish off, when accusing of hacks in game, let it be on the rules or not, it IS againt the rules, when accusing of hacks in game it allows the hacker to acknowledge that you are onto him and that a moderator might be coming soon so he can toggle his hacked client. When doing this it acts like a form of helping the hacker get away woth hacking and then continuing with ruining everyones game by hacking some more.

In the end, what my point is, please do not accuse of hacks in game and simply type in /report {player name} reason. It will help us moderators a lot and keep the Avicus Network a better place to bond with new people!

Best regards ~vladthegreat100
