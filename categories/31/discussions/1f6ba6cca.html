How To Tell If Someone Is Hacking. by Porkyyy August 10, 2014 at 2:08 AM UTC

Hello everyone! In this thread, I will be talking about some different hacks, and how you can tell if somebody is using one of them. In-game, I get called a hacker plenty of times (as a lot of you do as well), and this will hopefully show you how you can tell if somebody is using a hacked client.

1 - Aimbot / Killaura
The player never stops swinging their sword. They keep hitting other players nonstop, which inflicts damage upon multiple players at the same time. Their head sometimes will swing in the direction that they are hitting, which could be easily recognized. Also, they will be hitting you from a few more blocks away than they are supposed to, which makes it very easily recognized. *Report anyone using this hack.*

2 - Forcefield
This hack is probably the easiest one to catch. The player using this hack will be hitting others from many directions, even if the players are behind the hacker. This hack is very similar to aimbot, but the difference is that their armour can sometimes look quite glitchy, and they do not let players get within 1-2 blocks close to them. This hack acts as a type of circular "wall" between the hacker and the other players, which is what the main feature of this hack is. *Report anyone using this hack.*

3 - NoFall
The player using this hack will not take fall damage from any height. You are able to catch a player using this hack if you knock them off a high place (for example), and they do not die, nor do they take any fall damage. The easiest way to tell if someone is using this hack is if you knock them into the void, and they keep glitching back up to the point where you knocked them off. They will continually glitch vertically, which is clear cut evidence that they are using this hack. The easiest way to catch these hackers is if you record them. *Report anyone using this hack.*

4 - Fly / Antiknockback
If a player is floating in the air, moving around, that is because they are using a fly hack. This is the easiest hack to catch, and the way to report them is to catch them on video, or /report them, as a moderator will come. Regarding the Antiknockback, if you hit a player with an arrow / melee attack and they remain in the spot where they are, they are either using the Antiknockback hack, or they are lagging. This hack is not easy to catch, because some players could simply be lagging, and not using the hack. However, if a player is moving around, constructing a bridge (for example), and they get shot by an arrow, and take no knockback, that is how you can tell that they are using this hack. *Report anyone using this hack.*

5 - Regen Hacks
If a player is constantly being hit by multiple players, is not block hitting, and is barely moving, they are most likely using regen hacks (unless it's pot pvp). Sometimes a player will be lagging and will avoid being hit. *Report anyone using this hack.*

6 - Spider
If a player climbs a wall up without any ladders or blocks. It's really simple to catch.
You can see the hack also, if a player jumps on a wall and they are hanging there or it's looks like they are flying on the wall. *Report anyone using this hack.

In reality, there are some people who are very good at PvP, and they might get accused of using aimbot/killaura, but please read this thread before accusing anyone of hacking.
An example of this is glitchy1. He is very fast at clicking, and PvP in general. It may appear to some people that he is hacking, but he is a perfect example of someone who is just talented at PvP.

Please think before you accuse someone of hacking. For those of you who did not know, it clearly states in Section B, Number 4 of the rules that you must not publicly accuse others of hacking. That is against the rules, so before you accuse someone of hacking, think again.

1 - Spectate the player to confirm that they are hacking.
2 - Use /report (player name) (reason) to report the player.
3 - If a moderator does not come, record the player and post the report on the forums, under the section "Player Reports".

If you want me to add any more hacks to the list, provide me with a definition and I will add it!

Hopefully this helped a lot of you.
Happy Hunting Hackers!

-Porky.
