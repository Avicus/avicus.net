The real reason I retired / AMA by Crazy_ August 24, 2017 at 2:08 AM UTC

Alright. So the other day I retired from Admin. Not everyone knows my reasons for leaving, and since I imagine most people have made their own guesses by now, let's clear up the confusion.

I guess the biggest one that I've heard is that I retired because of something going on with the Admins, or something that I didn't agree with in the Admin team. To debunk this in the simplest way possible, I'm the sort of person that when faced with a problem in my work space, I work to fix it. Retiring for that reason would have been, in my opinion a waste of an opportunity. 

Second most common guess I've heard is kind of related to the first. I did not retire because of any one individual in an Administrative position. While there were definitely things I didn't like, it wouldn't cause me to retire - it goes back to the whole thing about how I like to fix the issues, plus it's not like you can't put your foot down and argue with reason and logic against something that has none.

So now that those two have been debunked, here's the real reason I retired. I've been on the Senior team since November. It started getting to a point a few weeks ago, where I started not enjoying spending countless hours resolving issues, maintaining internal services, and working on new projects. I kept the idea of retiring at the end of the summer in the back of my mind. Then the offer of Admin fell on the table, and I still wasn't sure what I wanted to do, so I figured I'd just take the position and see what comes from that. Now we're at that last few weeks of summer and I still feel like I wasn't enjoying my job as much as I did a few months ago. My first year of University starts in a few weeks, and with that comes a bunch of work and opportunities. I'd much rather be spending my time towards that sort of stuff, instead of Avicus.

These sort of retiring posts typically have like a thank you to all the people who made a difference in my time here. Quite honestly, there's too many to count. If you left any positive effect on me, you know who you are. I will give a special shout-out to Greg for getting me to join the staff team, to Posighdun for making me want to come back and for working with me as my Admin bud for several months, and to Josh for spending countless hours helping me out with the projects I worked on - it really goes a long way.

To the Avicus staff team, I wish you the best of luck, I love you guys. To the community, I hope you can bear with them as they try their best to make this the best server to be a part of <3

I'll incorporate an AMA into this as well, if you'd like to ask any questions. I'll try my best with those opinion questions, but there's a good chance that if we never spoke, I won't have much to say about you.

Best part of being admin? Part of the staff team?
I wasn't an admin for very long, and I didn't get to do anything really that I couldn't have already done as a Sr mod. So I'll split this answer into two parts. The best thing about being an Admin was how much more involved in the server I was. Being an Admin forces you into that position where you just have to be able to know what's going on around the server and deal with issues anyone might have. While this can be a pain sometimes, it was awesome since it really allows me to do more on the server, and not just focus on the mod team - though the mod team was always my biggest interest.

So the second part of this then, my favourite part of being a Sr was being able to do all the internal work needed to manage the server. There's seriously always things that can be worked on, and a lot that go unnoticed until they become issues. Like sure moderating is fun for the first few months, but then you start to feel forced to join the game and actually moderate - especially if you got bored of the game years before like I did. It was fun to be able to keep busy and do all those little things

My favourite part of being on the staff team in general was how involved in the community you get. I love being able to get to know people, and just all around be a part of it (as I'm sure some people have noticed if they've known me long enough ;p).
