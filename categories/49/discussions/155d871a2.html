How come you came to Avicus?

I first came to Avicus after hearing about it on some OCN thread a few years ago. Since then, I knew of the server, but didn't play much (I'd come to the lobby somethings to parkour though). It would have been about 2 summers ago, so 2015, where either Javi, Pel, or Vobaseball noticed me playing one day (again probably just parkouring in the lobby) and said hi. After about that point, I started to realize how much of a server Avicus actually was. Before that, I always just kinda thought of it as a server that started as an OCN clone, and then just didn't do as well.

I showed up some other time in the fall, and started to play then. Every now and then I'd check the forums. I saw ref apps open, and decided to apply (why not). After getting ref, I stayed here, and the rest is history.

Favourite food or drink?

I love apple pie. Oh, and tea.

Have you ever played an Avicus tourney, if so which one?

I've never really been big on tournaments. I haven't played an Avicus one, and don't really plan on it. I did briefly try to get into the competitive scene here, though that didn't last long.

Wow ok. I'm going to be honest here. When I first met you, you really struck me as a mod fanboy or suck-up. Best and worst part about being a senior?

Alright, so best part first. I love the work Sr is responsible for and involved in. My responsibilities aren't strictly based around moderating the server, which means I can do my work without having to play Minecraft, or even be on my computer.

I think the worst part was the timing. It made learning how to be a Sr quite difficult, especially since I was thrown into it all days after coming back from a 3 month retirement as a moderator. I had to be making decisions based on people's actions over the past days, but I had no idea how they acted.
Adding to the timing of it all, while I'm sure I'd do a better job now then I would have 3 months ago, I would have preferred to have been a Sr when I was actually more interested in the game itself. I came back to have a little more to do, and got a whole lot more instead (which doesn't mean I won't do a good job, don't get me wrong. It just changes my priorities up a bit).

Opinions of me?

You always stuck out to me as someone who can do very well working towards something you believe in, but quick to bash what you don't. As a former moderator you stuck out to me as someone who most definitely knows what he's doing in terms of moderation (which is something I like to see in staff).
