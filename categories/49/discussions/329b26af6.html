Do you have any tips on time management and high school?
First, I'll just tell you what my typical day looks like. In the morning, I usually wake up at seven and go to school at eight. School ends at 3:30 in the afternoon and I usually head straight to the library or nearest coffee shop to do homework and study. I spend a few hours there and get home at around seven or eight, and eat dinner. Then I'm left with a few hours to do whatever I want, and I usually use this time to practice music or work on programming stuff.

I'm gonna assume you're an incoming freshman. First of all, realize that your grades do matter and don't make the same mistake as I did by fucking up your freshman year. People say that colleges don't care about your freshman grades, but that's not true. Public schools, such as the UC schools, only look at your 10-11 GPA, but your grades all matter. People like to stack up on AP classes because it makes them look smart, but that can hurt you more if you're not careful. If you're just doing it for the college credits, you can also self-study the subjects. I self-studied six AP tests in my junior year and I found it much less time consuming than having to take an actual course, and I actually scored higher than my friends who took the class.

Second thing is don't procrastinate. In middle school, I was used to getting everything done the night before it was due, or even that morning. That won't work for high school, especially since you're taking those AP classes. Manage your time wisely. For English classes, make sure you read the literature thoroughly and not Sparknote it the lunchtime before the exam. For math and science classes, make sure you actually understand the content and not just memorize formulas and facts. KhanAcademy is a great resource if you ever get stuck on something or just need further clarification.

If you're like me and can't concentrate at home because of siblings and parents watching TV, do homework everyday after school at a coffee shop. It's a really chill environment and usually has fast wifi, and if you go there often enough and have a membership they'll usually let you get unlimited refills. I found it to be much more productive than just trying to work at home. If I need to study with a few friends for an exam, it also helps mainly because my parents don't allow me to have people over.

Last thing is to not spend all your time on schoolwork. You only have four years of high school, so be sure to live a life outside your room. There's a lot of social events such as football games, dances, and concerts that most schools offer so take advantage of those. I'm really tired right now; just got off a flight a while ago so I might not have Englished correctly. Good luck with the next four years.

What were some of the 6 AP classes that you could self study? During my junior year, I self-studied Environmental Science, Physics 1, Physics 2, Calculus BC, Chinese (doesn't really count since I'm a native speaker), and U.S. Government. My teacher for AP Statistics didn't teach at all though, so I basically self-studied that as well. I don't recommend trying to teach yourself Calculus BC since I started in September, but if you're really interested in math and are willing to devote a lot of time to it, then go for it. For Physics, make sure you fully understand concepts and not just memorize equations. Keep doing practice problems in a textbook, not a prep book. The test is conceptual and there was probably one problem that involved numbers, and it wasn't even an FRQ. Environmental Science is pretty straightforward; just memorize everything in the SmartyPants Study Guide and you're set. Government was doable since I follow politics very closely, but the Crash Course videos will teach you everything you need to know for the test. For statistics, just go through the Barron's book and you'll be fine. For every test, make sure to do practice FRQs. It's mandatory if you want to achieve a high score on the test.
Do you recommend taking naps? Yes! Take naps if you're tired. It helps way more to do homework after waking up from a two-hour nap than to sit through your homework not knowing what you're doing.
