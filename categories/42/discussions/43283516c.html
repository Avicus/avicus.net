Porkyyy August 17, 2016 at 4:08 PM UTC

Ok here I go:
1 - Have a good fps. Nobody wants to watch a video which is laggy, as they'll immediately close the video (trust me I know from experience)
2 - Have good funny commentary.. nobody wants to watch a boring video
3 - Know how to use editing software.. the more editing you put into your videos, the more people will like it. I personally use Sony Vegas Pro 12 but you can start out with windows movie maker if you aren't too familiar with editing yet
4 - Involve your audience in your videos in some creative way
5 - Don't record anything you don't enjoy.. if you don't like it, chances are the video won't be as fun to record and therefore you'll probably have not as good commentary and the video won't do well

There are TONS of other things that I could write but I'll leave you with these for now, since they're the basic essentials.

Oh and I'd recommend Camtasia Studio as a recording software. I am switching over to it and I have to say it's one of the most convenient to use.

Good luck! :)
