Fredrick Trump, Donald Trump's grandfather, was the one who was accused of changing his name. This, is in fact, false. Fredrick Trump on every official document is named Trump, and not Drumpf, contrary to popular belief. Here's an article in German that proves the counterclaim. It investigates the Trump name from a German movie that aired in 2014 (Kings of Kallstad). The confusion arises with the name's background. After Kallstadt was invaded by numerous countries, a lot of names had been changed over into new ones, even including first and middle names, as they were to act as new identities.

The first mention of the last name, Trump, ultimately was on page 26. The first mention of Trump stems from Donald Trump's great, great, great, great, great, great, great, great, grandfather. His name was Hanns Trump, who once lived in 1608 as a lawyer. You can look up numerous other sources that prove this claim and invalidate yours, that the initial name was Drumpf.
