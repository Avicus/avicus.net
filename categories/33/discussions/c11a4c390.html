A Question about Nebula Gameplay / Rules by Classical_freaK July 19, 2014 at 9:07 AM UTC

I've been an avid Nebula player for a long while, at least six months now. I'm on my way to Prestige 1, and I've seen a great deal of things from the game. I fell in love with the last-generation maps, like Senex and Rendezvous. In these maps, the spawn has special zoning that blocks any chance of spawn-killing, thus enforcing the rule in a very efficient way. How come so many of the new-generation maps (like previously discussed, Illegal Office) don't have spawn protection anymore? I feel that map makers need to have more restrictions and more importantly, more requirements of their maps.

This is only my opinion, but having a strict rule against spawn-killing yet not making it difficult to do so seems a bit contradictory to me. You say, "you cannot eat the forbidden fruit", yet you basically put it in their mouths. I am not much of one for spawn-killing myself, but if people can't even spawn-kill at all, there's not fear of infractions or unfair gameplay.

And another question, why has chat changed so much? I used to not be able to say any curse words and now it's free-reign. And people say horrible stuff, and I can't exactly say "no cursing!" because it's not like anything's stopping them. 99% of servers that have chat-restrictions in the rules also have it in the coding. Why did this change? It kind of goes back to the spawn-killing rule. What's the point of having rules when you're giving people more opportunity to break them?

I love Avicus, and I have no complaints, just these two suggestions about my favorite Nebula game.
