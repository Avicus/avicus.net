Sevoo July 18, 2016 at 1:07 AM UTC

You don't have to be incredibly good at PvP to be good at Avicus. Players like Merp who get really bad ping and aren't the best at PvP can really shine due to the fact that they don't focus on credits or kills or PvP, they focus more on the monument. They are sneaky and have lots of techniques other, better PvPers strive for. At the end everyone has their style and it's better to have a good understanding of how competitive minecraft PvP works before getting into things like ninja bridging or fast skybridging. Try to find where you like to be on maps and try to learn the maps. I've found it easy to rush a lot of maps from looking at the map in a layer type of organization. You could rush from the sides, the middle, the same from below or from high up. And you shouldn't be afraid to try new things if you aren't succeeding in what your doing, but that doesn't mean give up. 

Overall try to get in contact with a couple of good PvPers you see in game and ask them about the basics of PvP. Try to get a decent understanding of...

-Strafing and the different kinds of strafes

-Types of bridging

-Knockback increasing

-Comboing

-What FOV and sensitivity is right for you (you''l want to experiment a lot with this)

-Using the right items in the right place and time

But I always recommend going to other people as well because I'm not the best player in the world and I have things to learn as well.
