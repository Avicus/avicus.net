Cannon Guide (1.7) by Vobaseball12 February 15, 2015 at 3:02 PM UTC

Some of you have probably seen my defense guide. Since I'm also pretty good with cannoning, I thought I'd make a cannon guide also!

1. Mounting Blocks
Every cannon needs a mounting block. For myself, I usually use a fence post as a mounting block. This gives a medium/level angle shot, which is the most effective to most situations.
If you're looking to take out a skybridge, use a fence as a mounting block. This allows for a high angle shot that is useful for taking out skybridges.
Slabs. For most encounters, these are not the way to go. But if you want to hit a target lower than what your cannon is at, slabs are great! They fire at a low angle, which is perfect for taking out underbridges, etc.

2. Cannons!
I put all the pictures in an album at http://imgur.com/a/G8xso they are in order.

The first one is the basic cannon
This is the easiest cannon to make and fire, but not the best. To fire you simply push the right button, and then the left.

Next we have the whiskey cannon, a great line fire cannon! This is a great cannon because they are very easy to make, and you can make them very fast. I put the basic one first, and the upgraded one next.

The next cannon is the whip. (the one made out of cobble)
This cannon is a cannon on steroids. It packs quite a punch and can hit targets pretty far away.

The 4th class of cannons is the diagonal cannon.

There is an automatic version and manual version. I put the manual first, and the automatic second.

The next class is the Icarus (player launcher)

Icarus cannons are great cannons to launch your team over to the enemies' side.
The first Icarus is a basic one. This can't launch you very far, but it is good for short ranges.

The second Icarus is the GameMode 1 Icarus. It was designed by an OCN team for use in a tournament. A heads up, this requires 1 repeater

The last Icarus is the most difficult to build, but the best one. This is the CLAF Icarus. This Icarus was designed by xCLAFx on OCN. This requires 2 repeaters.

The last cannon, and maybe even the most important to know is the arc/skybridge counter cannon.

This is easy to make, and very variable, which allows you to change things to suit what you need. I'm going to give you the basic one. You can change the angle of the shot by adding fences, the amount of charges, etc.

Thanks for Reading! :)
