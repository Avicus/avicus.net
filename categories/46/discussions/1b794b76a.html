badgg August 25, 2015 at 10:08 AM UTC

This should be done first;
Develop some sort of a stable senior staff team. Not one who will retire one week, then return couple days after. Not one with Admins who constantly rotate within the space of a few weeks. The senior moderators need to be flexible, and able to work together - not try to get each other demoted (yes it happens). 

Outside the senior staff; the administrators need to emphasis and encourage that a moderator position isn't all about social power. Moderators are to moderate because they enjoy helping others, not for power. They need to start accepting their mistakes, but only to immediately improve short after. Not only that, they need to be approachable. Not immediately shoot down ideas with their own opinion. Just because they dislike the idea or that it has flaws DOES NOT mean it should be ignored. This links back to equality; just because you are a moderator doesn't mean you are above the rules; or you should treat others with authoritarian power.
