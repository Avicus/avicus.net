Silentdawnslight December 30, 2015 at 10:12 PM UTC

I'm really just learning stuff myself honestly. I get like 5-6cps on average, and my aim is horrible. Decent with a bow sometimes at least! xD I like trying to learn new stuff though, saw people doing that fast bridge and gosh it took me like a whole day of practicing to just get good enough to be functional! xD

A lot of it is practice, and to get a feel for things. Some basic tips:

If you're low ground, you take advantage of it. The reason there's an advantage is because your vision comes from the top of your character, so your reach extends from there (not from the middle xD). You basically want to keep as much out of their reach as possible.

If you're high ground, keep them back. Drop an arrow to knock them back before closing in so you can get the first hit and prevent them from getting you in a combo where you can't reach them. Try blockhitting, you want to knock them back down. Fall damage hurts!
 
 If you're on a small bridge, you want to get the first hit, and combo them off the side. Same thing here, you can use a quick arrow to knock them back before you close in for an advantage. Try to blockhit, keep yourself from being knocked off the side to your best ability, and try to hit the corner of them instead of just head on after you get in, so you don't just knock them backwards on the bridge. 
 
Getting behind your enemy helps a lot. Can't hit you if they aren't looking at you! I like to circle around them, I used to always be holding w sprinting at the enemy, but I find just holding the w or a key to circle can throw them off pretty well (But only after you're there, don't just sidestep to the enemy from 10 blocks away xD). This is fun with speed xD
 
 If you catch somebody who isn't paying attention, if there's a ledge knock them off as quick as possible! And if there isn't you want to get up close and jump start jumping and hitting them as you fall to get as many crits in as possible, and drop them before they notice you.

 Don't stay still when you shoot. (If they're looking at you) Even if you're on a little bridge, move around it as much as you can. Jump, walk forwards, backwards, turn around suddenly. Make it a pain to shoot you! 
 
 Those are tactics I can think of off the top of my head, to get better at the sword and bow it's just practice practice.
