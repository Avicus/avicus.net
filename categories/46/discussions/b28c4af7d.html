Cubition {Now choose your name!} by KadirGarib December 7, 2014 at 4:12 AM UTC


I thought I might as well post here, and as well share something that myself, and a few others have been working on over time.

In short, this is pretty much a sandbox game that has been in development since earlier this year, and though inspired my Minecraft. The game is completely open-source and is available to those whom are developers willing to contribute to such project as it's driven by the community ;). I'll do my best to keep this short and simple whilst including a couple screenshots from a couple months ago, as well as links to the source, and our forums.

About

Initially I drew the motivation as per this project's creation, where I had asked a friend of mine whom is a decent developer if he wanted to make a game that actually works, in which he had agreed on, and thus created the foundation of this project. At the time, I didn't not have much time on my hands to contribute nearly as much as @beeselmane had, so I decided to cover the expenses of the project (setting up a forums...etc.). I purchased a license for Xenforo, and was soon approached by the company, Shockbyte, in which had offered to host our forums, and I was delighted by the offer. This was continuing to increase the potential of Cubition.

Upon the release prior to Cubition, we'll also be revealing an FPS with our own little twist to it! This project is managed by the same team, and its first glimpse will be uncovered on Christmas day (or on New Year's Day)! I can't guarantee a release of this game quite yet, but I can assure you that I'll be working on this quite frequently.

I prefer screenshots over a video presentation, however since this is an FPS, and most people prefer seeing a quick game-play of it, I'll go ahead and make it a game-video demonstrating the different features in it.

Below is a QA answering questions that you may be curious about.

What language is this game written in?
This game is written in C++ using Ogre3D as the game engine, this is to help boost the performance levels (aside that I understand the physics behind C++ a lot better than I do with Java :P)

When will it be released?
I don't have a scheduled release date, sorry. But as of current plans, I do have intentions of releasing it somewhere between January and February. Note that I started developing this on my own over the summer, so it's come a long way already.

Would there be anything I need additionally to run the game?
It'll run fine on the default software most computers come with.

Will multiplayer be added?
Eventually, yes. It'll allow you to host your own servers and things like that (don't expect that right now ;).)

Will it be open-sourced?
I don't plan on open-sourcing it atm, for now its closed, until I do a code-cleanup and enhance it a bit.

If you're wondering what has been going on with Cubition, feel free to view my last announcement on our forums.

KadirGarib January 16, 2015 at 1:01 AM UTC

Let's clear some things up, as this thread has been quite for nearly a month.

I'll be honest right now, I was on edge of deciding whether or not to shut this project down for good. There were cues everywhere that it was dying anyways.

To confirm and finalize my decision on shutting this project down, I contacted the most current developers, and the developers whom resigned a while ago. Discussion had led us to decide NOT to close down the project but redo everything and start from ground up, this time with a real plan. The lack of organization had led to the downfall of Cubition, and with hopes that we don't make the same mistakes...again.

Knowing that we are going through a full re-code, don't expect a release anytime soon ;).

If you are looking into following up on this project with broad detail, feel free to visit our forums (http://cubition.net). We're renovating the entire project, not just development-wise, but forum management as well.

KadirGarib April 3, 2015 at 11:04 PM UTC

Just wanting to give another quick update, relatively important to those wanting progress.

We are now allowing user-name registration. For the Alpha Test, there will only be 100 slots, but that may change depending on the amount of traffic it gets.

In order to register an account, you must also have a forum account. The email that you register with for your forum account is the one that you must register with when registering for an Alpha account. This allows us to filter out those who create multiple accounts :(. The only way that this could be bypassed is if you create multiple forum accounts, each having a different email, if that's ever going to be the case, you will be banned from the forum and from the Alpha test.

The account page overall is about 80% done, and most of its features work perfectly fine.

tl;dr

Registering

1) Visit http://cubition.net

2) Create an account.

3) Confirm your email

4) Login to your forum account.

5) Hit the Account tab on the navbar.

You should be directed to http://auth.cubition.net

6) Hit the Register button at the top right

7) Fill out this form:

8) Hit register

9) You should be logged in, if not hit the Login button and login manually using your credentials.

Thanks, and enjoy your name ;)!
