Avicus Creepypasta! ( please read it!) by SpicyTunaRoll9 June 3, 2014 at 2:06 PM UTC

If you don't know what a creepypasta is, it's basically a short fictional story designed to scare people.
I never said this, but I have a burning passion for writing. I'm a writer, and I especially love writing horror. So, this is an Avicus creepypasta. Here you go :P Please no hate.  Don't just go "OH TEH IZ NOT SCARII U SUK -111111." 

The screen lit up. The Keys began to tap. The player excitedly rested his fingers on the mouse. Having had a long, tedious day, the player decided to play some minecraft multiplayer. Since his favorite server was currently under maintenance, the player reluctantly shifted his attention towards avicus. His computer was notorious for getting tremendous lag spikes as it was an old computer and had a high ping, from time to time his minecraft client would crash or flicker repeatedly. 
Still dubious that the gameplay would run smoothly, the player also opened Teamspeak in hopes that he would meet benevolent players that would come to aid and solve his lag problems. The Player simultaneously logged into the avicus server and the teamspeak. As the player hovered his mouse over the teamspeak in hope of searching friends, he noticed that they were many players in channel 6. However, something seemed to be wrong. The Channel 6 had 2 extra digits, making it appear as channel 666. Thinking to himself that it was just a tasteless prank, he greeted everyone in the teamspeak and asked which nebula they were in. The players responded that they were in a game of Quintus. The player became confused, because to his last memory Quintus was removed from the rotary. Ignoring his memories, he found himself spawned inside of the usual purple dome. Rushing for players, the player seemed to get very bad lag spikes and NCP. Several times while jumping down the lag would continue for what seemed like an eternity. This was not surprising however, considering the player's computer was very obsolete. After a few minutes of gameplay the lag began to increase tremendously to the extent where he could not shoot an arrow. The lag spikes became unbearable and his screen starting flickering, and ultimately he timed out of the server with the following message. 

Failed to homicidium,: java.net.ConnectionException:  timed out: life.

Huh? The player wondered. This word never seemed to appear before, nor did he know its meaning. Concluding that it was simply a bug, he restarted his Minecraft client. As he was restarting his client, he asked the players in the teamspeak if there was any lag. Uneasingly, the players all responded that there was no lag. 

This is where things began to get paranormal. 

"It's just my computer", he thought. Joining the same game, he procceded to grab iron and armor and he headed towards the frontlines near the towers tunneling inwards of the map in order of making a tunnel. He was suddenly interrupted by a drop that made him land in a middle of an unidentified pitch black tunnel. Absolutely blinded by darkness, the player turned his brightness settings to fullbright. Even so, the tunnel seemed to be covered in this eerie red lighting. Wondering what this was, the player turned around to find himself surrounded by stone blocks covered in blood particles that one would normally see when hitting an enemy player. Freaking out slightly, he saw a player that was cloaked in dark garments with pale white eyes marked by bloodshot dots at the end of the 4 x 4 tunnel. Having barely any visibility, the player's name hovering above was: "homicidium". Startled by the name, he frantically began shooting his arrows at the player. The arrows simply passed though the player as if he was made of air. The anonymous player began to slowly move towards the player's fov, his bloodshot eyes filled with desire for murder. As he gradually approached, he heard a faint whispering.
homicidium, homicidium, homicidium....
 The voice was very faint but he realised that this was not coming from the game, it was coming from the teamspeak. The player hovered over the players names, each to find their names arranged in the order of. H O M I C I D I U M. The noise began getting louder. The player began to panick. Frantically beginning to emit cold sweat and his brain becoming clouded, he said, "Shut up!!". He attempted to quit the teamspeak he just couldn't. It wouldn't budge. The whispering gradually became louder and louder as the anonymous player got closer and closer to the player. 
"SHUT UP!!! SHUT UP!! SHUT. UPPPP!!!!"
With his mental stability on the verge of cracking, the anonymous player suddenly lashed out at the player and screamed, flickered and timed out of the server in one instant. The player, still heavily sweating found the connection timeout  message saying, " Look Behind." Then as he slowly took a deep breath, he sensed a cold, pale breath breathing across his shoulder. Ever so gently, he heard the phrase,                                " homicidium".
