It's people like you who really put us in a bad light and make the staff more reluctant to listen to our pleas when you whine pretentiously about something a myriad of other players have been requesting. It's inconceivably egocentric and selfish to expect the server to accommodate solely for your wishes with little (if any) regard to anyone else's.

What's with the opposition to keeping support for 1.7? I agree that the server can't run on an old version of Minecraft indefinitely, but using the "all servers will inevitably switch to 1.8" argument is senseless. If a history textbook has two versions — both of which supply the same fundamental material — and the "newer" one presents it in a more irrational and inefficient format (true story), why "upgrade" from the older one? To digress, I've asked before why the need to drop 1.7 support was so exigent all of a sudden and never received an answer, but now, as far as I'm aware, there was some code for which 1.7 was too obsolete to utilize, judging by the recent downgrade. That, on the other hand, is a valid reason.

Anyway, I feel the same way about 1.8. Sure, having new blocks and items is cool... the new enchanting system is much more preferable as well. I very much like those facets of the update. But, at least when on Avicus, the PvP aspect of gameplay — what this server invariably revolves around in almost a subjugated, slavish manner — is completely butchered. I don't even need to delve much into this specific issue because it's been evinced countless times by others (Stimulating and Tino, to name a few), and the forums are already cluttered with enough threads on the subject.

I used to be one of the most active players in Nebula prior to the Omega Update; when said update was released, I played one match of DTM. Save for a (pitiably few) brief, intermittent instances, I have not been on the server since. This is entirely because I can hardly get a single kill anymore — I literally have to strike someone four times before they take damage. If I ever charge at a member of the enemy team headfirst, I always lose — even when in superior armor — because the game is practically weighted in their favor to grant them the first hit. After that, I either succumb to an inhuman combo, or fly off the map; this is all due to either (a) Avicus using 1.8, (b) poorer latency on the server's end, or (c) both.

That effectively makes Minecraft completely unplayable for me. Ultimately, retrograding is at best palliative; a temporary, relatively sufficient fix for a more divisive issue...
