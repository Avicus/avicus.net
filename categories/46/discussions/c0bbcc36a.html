Official Peripheral Recommendation Thread. by Sevoo March 11, 2016 at 4:03 PM UTC

Hi. It's about time someone made one of these, and I decided to do it! Think of this as the technical support thread, only for keyboards, mice, headsets/headphones, speakers and monitors/screens.

Here's a question list for every category for you to answer for me to give a good and unbiased recommendation that suits your needs.

Keyboard:

-Budget?

-Do you want a mechanical keyboard? If so, what switch type do you want? Here's a video explaining all the types of switches and what a mechanical switch is. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ps5DEeSBl5s

-Do you want macro keys?
-Do you want back lighting? If so, what color? You can get RGB lighting, which is basically 16.8 million colors to choose from for your back lighting.
-Do you want dedicated media keys? You can also have them on the F keys using the FN key if you'd like.
-Any more special features/ addons you would like? (ex. thumbster keys, windows lock key, USB hub, etc.)
-What games are you going to be playing?

Mouse:
-Budget?
-Do you want side buttons? If so, how many?
-What games are you going to be playing?
-Do you want lighting? If so, what color? You can get RGB lighting, which is basically 16.8 million colors to choose from for your back lighting.
-What is your mouse hand?
-Do you use claw, palm or fingertip grip? Note that some people use hybrid grips, like me. Here's a guide to the different grip types: http://www.epicgear.com/en/technologies/types-of-mouse-grip
-Do you like a light mouse or a heavy mouse?
-Do you constantly change between DPIs (Dots per Inch)/ Sensitivity?
-Do you like high or low DPI?
-What mice have you used it the past?
-Do you want a wired or wireless mouse?
-Any more special features/addons? (ex. hyper-fast scroll wheel, tilt scroll wheel, interchangeable mouse parts, etc.)

Headset/Headphones
-Budget?
-Do you need a microphone? If so, would you want for it to show when your not using it or hidden? Are you going to do recording with this microphone or just some Skyping for, let's say a comeptitive League match?
-Do you want lighting? If so, what color? You can get RGB lighting, which is basically 16.8 million colors to choose from for your back lighting. Take note that most headsets that have lighting are worse when compared to headsets with no lighting. Headsets can still look cool without lighting!
- Do you want a wired or wireless headset/headphones?
-Do you sweat or itch a lot around your ears? 
-Do you want an open design (you can hear your outside your game and the sound stage is a lot wider) or a closed back design (better for submersing yourself in games, but smaller sound stage)?
-Do you want to use your headphones publicly or strictly in your home?
-Do you want earbuds, an on ear or around ear design?
Do you want any special features/addons? (ex. 7.1 surround sound, in cable controls, audio amp, etc.)
-Do you prefer USB or audio jack?
-Will you be monitoring sound?
-What kind of music do you listen to?
Speakers:
-Budget?
-Will you be monitoring sound?
-Do you want a subwoofer?
-Do you want big or small speakers?
-What color do you want them to be?
-Will you be travelling with them a lot?
Monitors/Screens:
-Budget?
-How big do you want it to be in inches? (15 inches is small, 19 inches is better, 21.5 inches is medium, 24-28 inches is standard, 28+ inches is big)
-What games do you play?
-How much FPS do you get it the games you play?
-Do you want a normal, ultrawide or ultrawide AND curved monitor? Take note that ultrawides and curved monitors are expensive.
-Do you want a specific color?
-Do you have any lighting around where your monitor will be? If so, is it bright or just for mood lighting?
-Do you want an HDMI, DVI or VGA input? Here's a video explaining every type: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f38sotYHqtA
-Do you want a 60 hz, 100hz, 144hz, etc. monitor? This is completely synced with your FPS, so the refresh rate (hz) HAS to be in sync with your FPS for you not to experience screen-tearing. Here's a video explaining what a refresh rate is: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_PTp8isuJJs
I hope all of you find that one peripheral that is just...for you specifically!
