You have to look at very good players. Spectate good players when they're on. Learn how to strafe first into other techniques and keep building. Don't try to copy someone else's style, you form your own. I find the best PvPers are the ones who generally don't go for crits cuz when they do it makes it easy for the other person to strafe your hit, I punish people immediately if they try to crit me, I just strafe the hit and go behind them leaving them wide open to kill with a combo. From DGB you can take away how to combo and semicircle strafe. From Tino you get counter strafes, semi circle strafes and how to really lock someone in a combo. Learn all you can but I'm not giving away my PvP secrets :3. Or my combos. Check me out in game on a good day to see if you can catch me crit-dodging, strafing. My style isn't average, it's mainly taking huge advantages of mistakes and openings that people make which makes it really hard for me to play against Tino in specific because he counter-strafes so well there's virtually no mistakes or opportunies to capitalize on. I'm not going to give away everything now :P.

goldenwalrus909 October 11, 2014 at 12:10 AM UTC

When someone's facing you, block-hit, crit, and move to one side. Then, if their aim gets off you or you're getting low on health, sprint forward, shift in mid-air, and get a good knockback hit on them. After that, sprint towards them, moving more quickly to the side you previously chose, and strafe them. 
Using this, you're trading hits in the beginning, then you're finishing them off. I try to use it as much as possible :3
