Pavlo January 22, 2016 at 9:01 AM UTC

Of the ones you listed, the Oneplus X is noticeably weaker than the other two.

Here you make a choice between power and looks.

The Oneplus Two looks like quite a powerful beast. Two quad core processors, 3 gb of ram and a powerful GPU for a mobile. With a 1080p screen it's got quite a high pixel density which looks very nice. It also has a nice 13mp camera.

The Moto X 2015 has its own strengths. It also has 3 gb of ram, but instead of two quad core CPU's it has one quad core and a dual core. Despite this is still quite a hefty amount of processing that it can do. The GPU is also of a slightly older model from the Oneplus Two, however also still very powerful. What the moto x does have, is a very very nice 1440p screen, with a pixel density so high you literally won't see a single dot with your naked eye. It also has an impressive 21mp camera.

This is just the generally important specs of those two phones, but there's more you might like to take into account. Things like storage limitations, the OS layout and features, and many other smaller things.

Anyways, I hope this helped :)
