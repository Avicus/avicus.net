The ball took me like 30 minutes it's fairly straight forward to do, the building one took a couple of hours because it takes time to set up the building for one, then the explosion and character placement take a little while the render time was about 30 minutes on that one and then you get into compositing which can take a long time, in that case I had a fairly simple setup though so it only took around 30 minutes to make it all look how it does.

Of course then you can start doing more hyper realistic stuff where a single scene can take months of work from modeling to lighting to material creation (that's the real kicker) to very long render times.

LavaLeaker January 4, 2015 at 9:01 PM UTC

Teach me how please!
I can't personally teach you, but if you want I'll get you started. 

http://www.blender.org/ go there and download blender, it's the only free 3D software worth worrying about.

Bookmark this http://texturemate.com/ and this http://www.cgtextures.com/ you'll need them in the future for textures.

Go here to learn the basics https://www.youtube.com/user/cgboorman

Once you have the basics down good come here to start learning about some more advanced concepts http://www.blenderguru.com/tutorials/ and here if you have some money to spend come here as well http://cgcookie.com/blender

Make sure to watch these two videos right after you finish the beginner series as they will greatly assist you in understanding how everything works and what looks good. http://www.blenderguru.com/tutorials/understanding-colors/#.VKmoNCvF98E
http://www.blenderguru.com/tutorials/understanding-composition/#.VKmoMSvF98E

And of course if you ever need a blender community to talk to or just to see other people's work come here http://blenderartists.org/forum/

It'll be hard at first but don't give up and good luck!
