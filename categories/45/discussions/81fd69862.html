Hey everyone!
Max here with another strange problem. This one isn't necessarily math, but I would say its almost impossible to solve (even if its only a logic problem). I found this problem while reading BBC News: Its a problem Singapore used to measure teenagers ability to think logically (at least I'm pretty sure what it was... I know it was for teens), and it baffled the world. If you think you can solve this mind numbing puzzle, good luck!!!

"Albert and Bernard just become friends with Cheryl, and they want to know when Cheryl's birthday is. Cheryl gives them a list of possible dates. 
 
May 15                May 16            May 19
June 17               June 18
July 14                July 16
August 14           August 15       August 17

Cheryl then tells Albert and Bernard separately the month and day of her birthday respectively.

Albert: I do not know when Cheryl's birthday is, but I know Bernard does not know too.
Bernard: At first I don't know when Cheryl's birthday is, but I know now.
Albert: Then I also know when Cheryl's birthday is.

So when is Cheryl's birthday?"


Good luck on your endeavors! I hope someone gets it (Please do not look up or link content. Just guess and show logical reasoning).

-Max

Ok, since Albert told Bernard he (Bernard) doesn't know, it means it must be a month in which the date is similar to another (Bernard has options to guess from). Thus, the only pairs are [May 15, August 15],[May 16, July 16],[June 17, August 17], and [July 14, August 14]. Since June 18 is no longer an option, the updated list goes as follows: 
May 15                May 16            May 19
June 17          
July 14                July 16
August 14            August 17

As you can see, June now only has one option: the 17th, driving Bernard to state how he now knows.
