Edward Mycroft   Dear Diary,   The only thing I can do is moan and cry.  I’m trying to live these last hours I have until I cease from existence.  I feel as if I am in a living fire.  Even though I have a perfect amount of gold, it won’t matter now.  Besides, it took almost Death to get it.  Bandits beat me up and stole my treasure.  I got shot in my left leg...but worst of all I found the gold in the ravine.   A few hours ago, I found an enormous ravine.  Out of curiosity I peeked and looked.  I saw bits of shiny, yellow rock.  The only thing it could be was gold, so I did what anyone would do, I climbed down.  I was barefoot so my feet were in terrible pain.    As I went down, I thought this would be a good day for the first time- at least, until I slipped.  I fell and grabbed the nearest branch, I held onto with my life.  I looked down and saw that the ravine seemed ten times deeper.  When I thought things could not be worse, the branch broke.  I fell to what I thought would be my death, and for this I wanted to die.  I did not want to suffer and feel the after effects of the fall.  For sure, death would be next.  I woke up still alive.  “Why?” I asked.  “Why am I still alive”.  I could barely move and everything was numb.  I could not hear anything and I saw the blood around my body.     However, I had just remembered about the gold stuck to the wall.  I looked at the gold and realized I did not bring my pickaxe.  The only thing I had was you, my diary and a pencil.     Now at this moment, I hear the voices of the loved ones I will not be able to see no longer.  ”Come back,” the voices say.  The gold though, what I came here for, I will never be able to get it.  Bye bye Diary, I shall meet you in Heaven as I lay here dying, I know I will meet you.  

Hope you like!
