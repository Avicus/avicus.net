What I Can and Cannot Do for You by scolton April 8, 2015 at 2:04 AM UTC

I am a developer.  Developers write code.  As of right now (2015-7-4 21:56:50 EDT), the rank of Developer is the only one I hold, and nowhere in my job description does it say I am to do anything but write code.

As such, please:
  • Do not message me via any contact method that I have provided the public and ask me to appeal a punishment or unban you.  Any offenders will be immediately blocked from that form of communication.  Protocol states to either a) post under the "Appeal an Infraction" category, or b) contact Avicus staff at support@avicus.net.
  • Do not ask me to spectate someone and decide if they're hacking.  I have no qualifications as a moderator and quite frankly, I cannot see the difference between someone that is very good at PvP and someone who is hacking. /report works very well for these purposes; I assure you that you know more than me.
  • Do ask me questions about coding.  I love to help people learn.  I believe that coding in any language is a very important real-world skill that will soon become mandatory for any functioning member of society.  I will even teach you an entire language.
  • Do give me suggestions via any channel that you find on my profile.  Do not give me suggestions in game.  They will be ignored.  Suggestions must be given in such a way that I can catalog incoming suggestions, and in-game chat is transient and I will quickly lose your suggestions.
  • Do not ask me for credits in game.  Come on guys, you saw what Kylo did to me when I gave myself credits, what do you think he would do to me if I gave them to you?  You might never hear from me again.
  • Do ask me for creative gamemode if and only if you are in the hub.  I am authorized to give people creative gamemode in the hub, for the time being.
  • Do continue to be supportive of me and the network as a whole.  Overall, the community has been very helpful at getting me situated in this network.
Thank you very much.
