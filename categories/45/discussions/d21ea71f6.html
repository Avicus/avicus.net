Crazy_ March 13, 2016 at 7:03 PM UTC

Unfortunately checking people's ranks and profiles doesn't work all the time. Someone could be hacking who has a rank, or has a lot of playing time/kills/etc. Or it could be the other way around and a new player who has no rank, and just joined (who is not hacking), is being accused of hacking.

If you don't want to get an infraction for hackusating (accusing players of hacking) a player in chat, then just don't say "PlayerXXX has kill aura". Instead use that time to type in "/report PlayerXXX hacking - kill aura (head is twitching in a group of players when fighting)". If a mod is online, then he'll/she'll come and save the day. 
Also, in terms of reports, I'm not sure how they work on Avicus, but on OCN, it's so much nicer to get a descriptive report. So instead of reporting someone for "hacks" or "cheats", you could report someone for "forcefied - hitting players all around him" or "noslowdown - running with a drawn bow".

Also, if you want to investigate, the best thing to do is join obs, and boot up that recording software. Get some evidence of him/her hacking, and upload it to the forums (https://avicus.net/forums/categories/47). If a mod comes on and bans him before you upload your footage, no biggy, you don't have to submit the online report. But if no mod comes (maybe no mod is online), then you just got a hacker banned.
