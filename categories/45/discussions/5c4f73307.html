I think the best way to fix this is to make a system where donors can't change the amount of players on a team by /join *team* by any more than 20%. This means if there's a match with 5v5, if you and your donator friend tried to join, both of you couldn't because you're changing the amount of players on that team using /join *team* by over 20%. If you're transferring 5 to 6, you're increasing 5 by 20%. This means when you're /join *team*ing, you're changing the player amount by 20%. Your friend then has to do play on the other team because if (s)he /join *team friend is on*, she'd increase the team amount by 40%-- you're going from 5 to 7. This can also be shown as an inequality. (If the amount of donors using /join *team* is x, and the amount of players on the other team is y,) x ≤ .2y. If there are 5 people on a team, this means only 1 can join (1 ≤ 1).
