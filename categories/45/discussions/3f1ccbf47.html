I imagine, however with few personal experience, that normally when you retire you never know when you'll come back, if you ever do. You either feel burnt out, a serious family issue, some friend of you died, school work is starting to pile up, and the list goes on and on and on.

In my opinion, the "temp-retirement" definition is the same as "retirement" except its end, that isn't infinite Hence, any retirement can easily turn into a temporary one by just ending it before. Para temp-retired, and that's a fact, he left and he came back; you really can't call that infinite but temporary.

Obviously, those rage moments that make you say "who cares" and step down, even if the next day you were already regretting your decision, used to happen way to often leading to the headache of various senior staff members who had to handle the promotions/demotions log, give the rank ingame, teamspeak, maybe some kind of training or trial, etc. That's why it got limited to one, though I guess it's more of an indication for people not to temp-retire a lot rather than a severe "you are not getting mod again". We all know life can make us go through rough patches several (and some times, not so spaced) times. If someone lost a family member and decides to step down, because he can't keep up, then some months later decides he can step up again, then loses another friend or whatever and he really can't carry on with it, I really doubt we would be such a dick as for to say "nope, we are not giving the rank back to you" when he feels ready again. But it's really useful for people not to "rage-quit" for a couple days, at least by the looks of it.

If the player has already shown he is capable of being a mod, and has the required talent for it; but it's just that he decided he had to step down, we are not going to make him go through applications and hand-picking and all that, obviously.

To me it's very understandable, though I can see where you are coming from.
