I'm going to bring up the elephant in the room.

Making this thread does not reflect the staff team in a good light- virtually no other server would have moderators cling to the community on how to recognize their own flaws, when self-reflection should be an imminent trait once you become moderator. So, I choose to skim past that: I see that making such a thread makes you look insecure about your moderation, and furthermore in my perspective this is more or less a pull for attention to show the community that you actually want to do something to improve, yet you can't quite diagnose those issues yourself.

Considering the point that there's a lot of moderators and almost no senior mods, there is the chance that this thread was made to possibly have you look as to fill a vacancy within the team. If you get senior- congratulations! But I feel that you shouldn't be worried about the basics of being a moderator, and rather ask the question to yourself: How can I go beyond being a moderator? How can I exceed the senior's standards?

Your responses seem like "I can do that" (in response to suggesting better things for the server) and "I would gladly do that" (taking leadership initiative), but those should be givens. They should be things you're already doing as a representative of the server.

If you ask me, since you want my opinion: you should just straight up ask the seniors for feedback, if they have any from paying attention to you. Ask them to be blunt, upfront and honest about their opinions of you and how you do. Don't take "well" for an answer.

Sorry if this is too harsh, but I just don't like the concept of this thread.
