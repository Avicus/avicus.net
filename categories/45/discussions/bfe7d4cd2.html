Clijmart sad :( by Clijmart January 7, 2018 at 10:01 PM UTC

I remember when my train got to it's final destination, but I didn't want to get out.
I stayed in my seat, til the train started again and went back to where it came from.
As weird as it felt, I enjoyed it, and it was worth the extra time.

As much as this may be the final destination of this Avicus train, we can always go back or atleast remember where we've been before.
It may be sad, but after all the stations and places we've passed, there is always a final one.
Luckily there's lots of different tracks left to take, and when there's too little, more get created.
But the tracks you travel on the most, you will always remember...

I feel like it's worth looking back, trying to remember the amazing places we've traveled by.
From little moments and achievements, such as an amazing game of DTM. To bigger ones like tournaments and great updates and events.

This isn't as much of an AMA, but more of a place where we can ask questions, and you can answer whatever question asked.

I'll start of with a few, answer if you want or ask your own.
- What was your biggest accomplishment on Avicus? What are you most proud of?
- What will you be doing after this, and what things you learned on Avicus will you take with you?
- Which players do you really not want to lose contact with, after the shut down?


I want to thank everyone for the amazing experience and the awesome moments.
I've met lots of great people, and learned how to deal with the ones I didn't find too great.
I tried making a list of players to thank, but it got too long so I gave up :(
Just know that if you did any map development or building for Avicus, you're most likely in it.

Hope y'all have an amazing experience after this, and have had great moments on here, just like me.

Thanks for all,
Clijmart

Twitter - @Clijmart

My questions to you:
Why have you stayed all this time? What interested you in maps? How are you feeling?
I stayed mainly because I felt like I could learn a lot from the players. Building, XML/Mapfigging and moreover some social skills.
I also loved being able to help players. I tried to support mapmaking as much as possible, while always being honest.

What I love about the map making really isn't just the building. I love trying out new things, both buildwise and gameplaywise.
I tried to predict the gameplay of my maps, before they would get played and edit them if I expected flaws.
As little as most of my maps were used, most of them followed my exact gameplay plans, which I guess is a good thing.
(Please don't talk about 'The Toys', it was shit I know...)

I'm feeling great lately. Really busy IRL, but Minecraft is always high on my priority list.
Stupid as it sounds, it did help me get through depressing stages in life and I improved my autistic social skills by doing so.
I'm working hard on what I enjoy doing, and everytime it pays of a little, it motivates me even more :)
