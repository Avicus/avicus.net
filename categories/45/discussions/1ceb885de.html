Conflict of Interests by Numberz_ December 7, 2016 at 9:12 PM UTC

Hi there,

I was looking through the forums and came across Zac's post. Which actually brings up a topic I've been meaning to start a discussion about for a while now... conflict of interest. What is that? It basically means that if you have an interest (part of something ex. staff or team) you cannot take part in the discussions that directly give your interest a better advantage. That being said, this happens a lot on Avicus. Too often. 

Majority of maps for this tournament - made by one team that is competing in the same tournament.

Officials impacting the outcome of maps they play in. 

Staff that are in the same team as another player not being punished. 

These are just a few examples I can think of off the top of my head but there are probably a lot more. I know that humans cannot fix the bias behaviours towards friends, teammates and fellow staff members, but they can take steps to cut back the amount it happens. 

Simple steps I think could work: 
Having official decisions in tournament's made by another official that isn't playing in that match.  And not allowing the official that is playing in said match have any say on the outcome one-soever (same with refs). 
Picking a wider variety of maps for tournaments, not maps all made by a specific group (actually ask for map submissions in advance...). 


I would love to hear what you guys think.
