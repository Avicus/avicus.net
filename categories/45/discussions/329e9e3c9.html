Players. Most important. by hasl December 27, 2014 at 10:12 AM UTC

Owners. Their job is the hardest. They have to pay to keep the server running. Control the console. And advertise. Have ideas and devotion to the server. And more

Developers. Their job is to code. Do all the complex stuff to make a server what it is. Use plugins or even create their own. You can't learn the skills over night you study them.

Map Developers. It's all in the name

Administrators. Same jobs as mods just higher up. A lot of pressure and expectations. They have to use their powers wisely.

Moderators. Their job is to help. Help and support. Help, support and create an enjoyable experience and community for a Minecraft server. One of the most wanted places on a server. Wonder why?

Players. That's a majority of you. And you have the hardest job of all: Being online. What's the point of a server full of staff and no players? So many people say Mod is better, but they can't help unless you play! Players, everyone single one of you, are the most important factor of a server.

Now, just because you don't have powers like the people above you, does that mean you can't do their job as passion. You can't tell a player what's right and wrong? What's good and bad? Well, servers are different, so they have their own rules. Can you act as a mod, just without the powers? Just like a Map Dev.

An Avicus player_

Although there are some things that could be fixed, I like this thread. I made one on the old Avicus forums a while ago that was somewhat similar, and I still go by it. Players, whether donors or not, moderators or not, have a huge impact on the server, with all of their actions. Join the forums, make one (good quality) forum post; You've made the forums a better place. Log on the server, join a Nebula game, report a hacker; You've rid the server of a hacker, making it a better place. The little actions go a long way.
