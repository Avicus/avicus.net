MLGtino February 7, 2015 at 12:02 AM UTC

Ok:

1. Find the pack you want to use as a canvas. Usually you should use something like Faithful as a resource pack to add everything to. This pack is just serving as a spot to hold all of your swords and needs. You can change the name, description, and items, don't worry.

2. Open this pack with Winrar. Go to "assets", then hop on to "minecraft", then into "textures". Once you've done that, you'll see an ass load of files. Each has its own properties. GUI has chest inventories, your inventory, your crosshair, hearts, etc. Items has items such as swords, food, etc. Particles are self explanatory. Just look around in the other files and double click them to display the image. It's fun looking through them to see what each thing is :)

3. Do the exact same thing as step 2, only open the resource pack in which you would like to add a feature from. Now you should have two tabs open: one for Faithful, and one for the resource pack you'd like to take your item from (whether you want the sword, inv, etc). 

4. Now for the simple part. Let's say for example, you like the diamond sword in Sphax and you want it in your pack. Go to "items", press "d" on your keyboard (if everything is not in alphabetical order, click the small arrow above all your files to put it in order), then you should look and find "diamond_sword". Do this for both tabs. This part is really simple: Drag the diamond sword from Sphax and put it into the Faithful pack. Simple as that. This is the exact same thing for any picture of any pack of any kind. 

Any problems?
