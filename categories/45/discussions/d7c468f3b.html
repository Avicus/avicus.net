potatoarmor March 4, 2016 at 6:03 AM UTC

In my opinion, what killed Walls was:

1) New maps seemingly every week.  Some were nice, some weren't, but if you didn't know the map how were you supposed to do any good?  People weren't doing well and lost interest.

2) Lowered height limit.  If you can shoot somebody in the max height skybase, while you are standing on the ground, the height limit is too low.  And it is too low.  Raise it by 30 or 40.

3) Stupid, stupid rules.  "Oh, you shot across the walls before wall drop, you will be banned".  Stupid stupid stupid.  If you don't want people to shoot across walls then build the walls higher.  Frankly, now I often play on a similar game on a different server (bridges) where shooting across the water before bridge drop is a part of the game, as it should be in walls.  Make and enforce stupid rules and people will stop playing.

Then you have some mods who have no clue, like the one who was threatening to kick me for telling my teammate in Walls what the enemy was doing (as I was playing the game), he said I wasn't supposed to be giving tactical information.  As I was playing. 

I remember when there would be 10 players per team on a Saturday afternoon.  Those were the days.

There used to be a game on Avicus where you would spawn on a random map and you had something like 5 minutes before you could start attacking other players.  Normally you would look for iron for a sword and some armor, then it was a pvp battle or you could sneak up on people while they were mining.  Maybe bring back that game?

And why was Prestige taken away?  Why not keep it?  What about the kits you could buy for $1.99?
